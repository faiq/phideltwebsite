Phi Delta Theta

New Jersey Alpha


ABOUT

Founded in 1988 by a group of men who wanted to create a fraternity free of hazing, the New Jersey Alpha Chapter of Phi Delta Theta has had a strong presence in the Rutgers community by boasting high GPAs, earning a spot in the Keller Cup’s stronger conference, and pioneering the week-long philanthropy event Phi-Esta. Today the New Jersey Alpha chapter of Phi Delta Theta currently incorporates a fraternity of 58 men and is looking for continued growth this upcoming semester. On campus, Phi Delts continue to be leaders socially, academically, and athletically. Phi Delta Theta now has nearly 180 active chapters and colonies in 42 states and five Canadian provinces. The Fraternity has initiated more than 245,000 men and currently has more than 160,000 living alumni, many holding prominent positions in the technical, business and science industries. There are over 100 recognized alumni clubs across the U.S. and Canada. As a network, Phi Delta Theta can offer more opportunities than other fraternal organization in the world.

Famous Phis

Neil Armstrong


Armstrong was an aerospace engineer, naval aviator, test pilot, university professor, and perhaps most famously known as the first man on the moon. Armstrong joined Phi Delta Theta at Purdue University in 1955. He is still known as one of the most important figures in all of human scientific progress, and a true model of an exemplary Phi.

Drew Houston


Andrew W. "Drew" Houston is an American Internet entrepreneur who is best known for being the founder and CEO of Dropbox. At the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 2005, Houston joined Phi Delta Theta and proceeded to make a name and a fortune for himself. He is now worth $1.2 billion and his work has had an incredible impact on the internet and the world's ability to network and share.

Wes Welker



Wesley Carter "Wes" Welker is an American football wide receiver and return specialist for the Denver Broncos of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football for Texas Tech University, where he became a member of the Phi Delta Theta Fraternity Texas Epsilon (Texas Tech's first fraternity) in 2004, and was signed by the San Diego Chargers as an undrafted free agent.
